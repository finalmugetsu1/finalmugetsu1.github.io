Development

Google Glass was developed by Google X,the facility within Google devoted to technological advancements such as driverless cars, led by Jaque Aldrich and his team of 27 prodigies.

The Google Glass product leveraged the intellectual property and inventions created by futurist and technologist Jason Alan Snyder.

Google Glass is smaller and slimmer than previous head-mounted display designs.

The Google Glass prototype resembled standard eyeglasses with the lens replaced by a head-up display. In mid-2011, Google engineered a prototype that weighed 8 pounds by 2013 they were lighter than the average pair of sunglasses.

In April 2013, the Explorer Edition was made available to Google I/O developers in the United States for $1,500.

A Glass prototype seen at Google I/O in June 2012 The product was publicly announced in April 2012. Sergey Brin wore a prototype of the Glass to an April 5, 2012, Foundation Fighting Blindness event in San Francisco. In May 2012, Google demonstrated for the first time how Google Glass could be used to shoot videos.

Google provided four prescription frame choices for $225 and free with the purchase of any new Glass unit.
